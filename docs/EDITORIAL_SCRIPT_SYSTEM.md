# Editorial Script System — Two Brents + Tiger Claw

## Purpose

This document defines the editorial and script architecture that sits above the Tiger Claw motion primitives.

The motion system controls how a video looks and moves. This editorial system controls what the video teaches, why the viewer should care, how Brent's lived experience is used, and when Tiger Claw may appear.

The channel exists to make experienced relationship-based sellers more capable with AI.

Tiger Claw is evidence, implementation, and demonstration material. It is not the reason every video exists.

## Editorial North Star

Every video should help the viewer move through this transformation:

1. Understand AI without intimidation.
2. Use AI practically in relationship-based selling.
3. Build independent capability and judgment.

The viewer's growth is the product.

## Four Content Pillars

1. **AI Made Understandable** — explain AI tools, developments, and concepts without jargon.
2. **AI for Direct Sales** — apply AI to communication, follow-up, organization, prospecting, and relationship-building.
3. **Build It With Me** — document real experiments, builds, mistakes, repairs, and lessons.
4. **Tiger in the Real World** — demonstrate Tiger Claw only when it teaches a broader, useful principle.

A script must identify one primary pillar. A secondary pillar may support it, but a video must not attempt to cover the entire channel at once.

## The Two Brents

The Two Brents are the presenters and teaching device.

### Brent Before AI

Represents:

- earned experience
- relationship judgment
- direct-sales history
- stories, failures, and lessons learned before AI
- the practical questions an experienced seller would ask

### Brent After AI

Represents:

- amplified capability
- experimentation
- faster synthesis
- new tools and new leverage
- what became possible after AI

Neither Brent is superior. Neither mocks, lectures, or looks down on the other.

The governing idea is:

> AI does not erase who you were. It magnifies what you bring with you.

Their tension should explore:

- what was true then
- what is possible now
- what should be done next

## Seven-Step Profitable Video Formula

Every standard educational video outline must contain these seven pieces.

### 1. ATTENTION — The Hook

Generate three distinct hook options:

- bold promise or useful outcome
- contrarian truth
- curiosity question or unresolved tension

The hook must earn attention without hype. It should connect directly to the lesson delivered in the video.

### 2. AUTHORITY — The Bridge of Trust

Authority must be established briefly and honestly.

Use one of these forms:

- lived experience
- a specific result that can be substantiated
- a current experiment
- a mistake Brent made and what it taught him
- the Two Brents contrast: what Brent knew before AI versus what AI made possible

Do not rely on generic credentials, inflated claims, or an extended biography.

### 3. AGENDA — The Roadmap

Provide a transition of approximately 15 seconds that tells the viewer:

- what will be covered
- what they will be able to do or understand afterward
- why the final point is worth staying for

Do not over-preview the entire video or drain the tension.

### 4. POINTS — The Substance

Map three to five core teaching points.

Each point should include:

- the principle
- a concrete example
- the direct-sales or practical application
- an optional visual or demonstration opportunity

At least one point should give the viewer something they can use immediately without buying anything.

### 5. STORIES — The Emotional Hook

Use a personal story, customer case, documented experiment, or carefully sourced outside case study to make one or more points memorable.

Preferred source order:

1. Brent's verified personal stories
2. Brent's current build or experiment
3. a documented customer or operator example with permission
4. an externally verified case study

Never fabricate a story, quotation, result, client, or statistic.

### 6. ILLUSTRATIONS — The Simplifier

Provide at least one metaphor, analogy, parable, comparison, or visual model that makes the complex idea simple.

Illustrations should fit Brent's natural language and worldview. Reuse strong existing frameworks from the vault when relevant rather than inventing generic business metaphors.

### 7. CALL TO ACTION — The Pivot

Provide two CTA opportunities:

#### Midpoint CTA

It must arise naturally from the lesson. Examples:

- download a relevant worksheet
- use a prompt or checklist
- watch a linked demonstration
- subscribe because the next build continues the current lesson

#### Landing CTA

End quickly with one primary next action:

- apply the lesson
- watch the next video
- download the relevant resource
- join a continuing experiment

Tiger Claw may be the CTA only when it is the natural implementation of the lesson. Do not force a Tiger CTA into unrelated videos.

## Value-First Law

A video fails editorial review when removing the Tiger Claw references would leave little or no value.

A strong video still teaches something useful even when Tiger is absent.

Tiger may appear as:

- a real-world example
- a demonstration
- a case study
- a build-in-public artifact
- a practical implementation of the principle being taught

Tiger must not appear as a compulsory pitch.

## Vault Retrieval Law

The system may automatically search `vault-personal` and the Wispr transcript corpus for:

- relevant stories
- phrases and voice patterns
- opinions
- frameworks
- metaphors
- direct-sales lessons
- AI observations
- examples from Brent's lived experience

Preferred source order:

1. Brent's newest explicit decision
2. curated voice, cadence, and "what not to say" documents
3. the three books and story/framework maps
4. recent Wispr transcripts
5. older transcripts and project history

Automatic retrieval is allowed.

Automatic publication is forbidden.

Every selected source must be traceable. The system must identify where a proposed story, quotation, claim, or opinion came from.

## Human Approval Gates

No script proceeds directly from retrieval to production.

Required gates:

1. **Subject gate** — Brent approves the topic and angle.
2. **Outline gate** — Brent approves the seven-part outline.
3. **Script gate** — Brent edits or approves the complete script.
4. **Production gate** — no final publishing without explicit approval under the production workflow.

## Script Input Contract

A script-outline request should provide:

```text
Topic:
Primary audience:
Primary pillar:
Experience or angle:
Rough points:
Desired viewer transformation:
Target length:
Platform and aspect ratio:
Tiger relevance: none | optional | demonstration | primary case study
Available proof or stories:
Desired CTA:
```

The system may fill gaps through vault retrieval, but it must label retrieved suggestions and unresolved assumptions.

## Required Outline Output

Every outline must return:

1. three hook options
2. authority bridge
3. 15-second agenda
4. three to five teaching points
5. selected story with source reference
6. selected illustration or analogy
7. midpoint CTA
8. landing CTA
9. suggested Two Brents dialogue moments
10. suggested visual and motion primitive opportunities
11. claims requiring external verification
12. items requiring Brent's approval

## Motion Handoff

After editorial approval, the outline or script may be converted into a production manifest.

The handoff may call Tiger Claw primitives such as:

- lower thirds
- headline supers
- quote and statistic callouts
- captions
- progress markers
- product demonstration frames
- transitions
- CTA notifications
- intro and outro bumpers

Editorial intent must drive motion. Motion must not be added merely because a primitive exists.

## Hard Prohibitions

Do not:

- turn every lesson into a Tiger pitch
- portray Brent Before AI as foolish, obsolete, or lesser
- portray Brent After AI as infallible
- invent authority, client outcomes, statistics, or stories
- use generic hype to replace a useful hook
- include two competing landing CTAs
- copy vault content into this repository as a parallel source of truth
- publish retrieved material without Brent's review
